91 ///A not-necessarily-disjoint collective of boxes.
92 /**
93  A BoxLayout is a collection of Box objects that are assigned
94  to process numbers. Each box is associated with only one
95  process. Processes are numbered from 0 to n-1 (for a job with
96  n processes).
97 
98  A BoxLayout can be either open or closed.
99 
100  Open BoxLayout:
101  - Created by null construction or deepCopy.
102  - Boxes may be added to it.
103  - Non-const operations may be performed on the boxes in it.
104 
105  Closed BoxLayout:
106  - Created by constructor with vectors of Boxes and processors given explicitly.
107  - Cannot be modified.
108  - Represented as sorted boxes.
109  - Many uses of BoxLayouts require a closed BoxLayout.
110 
111  <b>Ref-counting</b>
112 
113  BoxLayout is an explicitly ref-counted object.
114 
115  Assignment and copy are compiler-generated. They increment the refcount
116  on the contained data members. They perform shallow, ref-counted
117  operations.
118 
119  Refcounting is a process whereby multiple instantiations make
120  use of a single implementation of that object and keep a tally
121  of how many instantiations are sharing. Thus:
122  <PRE>
123 
124  BoxLayout b1(boxes, procIDs);
125 
126  b1 ----> refcount = 1
127  ----> m_boxes
128  ----> m_processors
129 
130  BoxLayout b2(b1)
131 
132  b1 ----> refcount = 2 <---- b2
133  ----> m_boxes <----
134  ----> m_processors <----
135 
136  BoxLayout b3;
137  b3 = b2;
138 
139  b1 ----> refcount = 3 <---- b2
140  ----> m_boxes <----
141  ----> m_processors <----
142  ^^^
143  |||
144  b3
145  </PRE>
146 */
